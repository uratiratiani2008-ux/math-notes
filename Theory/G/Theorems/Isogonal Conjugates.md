Let $ABC$ be a triangle and $P$ any point not collinear with any of the sides. There exists a unique point $P^*$ satisfying the relations:
$$\angle BAP = \angle CAP^*, \quad \angle ACP = \angle BCP^*, \quad \angle CBP = \angle ABP^*$$
if two of the angle relations hold, then so does the third. 
$AP^∗$ is isogonal to line $AP$ with respect to $\triangle ABC$.

**(Isogonal Ratios)**. Let D and E be points on BC so that AD and AE are
isogonal. Then:
$$\frac{BD}{DC}\cdot \frac{BE}{EC}=\left(\frac{AB}{AC}\right)^2$$
