Symmedian - isogonal of a median
Let $X$ be the intersection of the tangents to $(ABC)$ at $B$ and $C$. $AX$ is a symmedian.
+ $KA$ is a $K$-symmedian of $\triangle KBC$
+ $\triangle ABK$ and $\triangle AMC$ are directly similar.
+ $\frac{BD}{DC}=\left(\frac{AB}{AC}\right)^2$
+ $\frac{AB}{BK}=\frac{AC}{CK}$
+ $(BCX)$ passes through the midpoint of $AK$
+ $BC$ is the $B$-symmedian of $\triangle BAK$, and the $C$-symmedian of $\triangle CAK$
+ $BC$ is the interior angle bisector of $\angle AMK$, and $MX$ is the exterior angle bisector.

let $ABCD$ be a cyclic quadrilateral. following are equivalent:
+ $AB*CD = BC*DA$
+ $AC$ is an $A$-symmedian of $\triangle DAB$ and a $C$-symmedian of $\triangle BCD$
+ $BD$ is a $B$-symmedian of $\triangle ABC$ and a $D$-symmedian of $\triangle CDA$
+ $ABCD$ is a harmonic quadrilateral.
