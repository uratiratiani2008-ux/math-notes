--
**harmonic bundle\harmonic**: $(A,B;C,D)=-1$. 

[[Lemma 9.8 (Midpoints and Parallel Lines)]]
--
+ $M$ is midpoint of $AB$, then $(A,B;M,P_\inf)$ is harmonic.

[[Lemma 9.9 (Harmonic Quadrilaterals)]]
--
Given point $P$, a circle $\omega$, tangents $PX, PY$ and point $A$ on $\omega$. 
+ $AXBY$ is a harmonic quadrilateral. 
+ $AQBP$ is a harmonic bundle.

[[Proposition 9.10 (Inversion Induces Harmonic Bundles)]]
--
+ Let $P$ be a point on line $AB$, and let $P^∗$ denote the image of $P$ after inverting around the circle with diameter $AB$. Then $(A, B; P,P^∗)$ is harmonic.

[[Lemma 9.11 (Cevians Induces Harmonic Bundles)]]
--
Let $ABC$ be a triangle with concurrent cevians $AD, BE, CF$ (possibly on the extensions of the sides). Line $EF$ meets $BC$ at $X$ (possibly at a point at infinity). Then $(X, D;B,C)$ is a harmonic bundle.
![[Harmonic Bundles 2.png|400]]

[[Lemma 9.12 (Complete Quadrilaterals Induces)]]
--
Let $ABCD$ be a quadrilateral whose diagonals meet at $K$. Lines $AD$ and $BC$ meet at $L$, and line $KL$ meets $AB$ and $CD$ at $M$ and $N$. Then $(K,L; M,N)$ is a harmonic bundle.
