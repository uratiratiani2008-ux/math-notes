Given 4 collinear points $A,B,C,D$, or four lines $a,b,c,d,$ concurrent at point $P$ cross ratio is:
$$(A,B;C,D)=\frac{CA*DB}{CB*DA}=\frac{(c-a)(d-b)}{(c-b)(d-a)}=(a,b;c,d)=\pm \frac{sin\angle(c,a)*sin\angle(d,b)}{sin\angle(c,b)*sin\angle(d,a)}=P(A,B;C,D)$$

**Cross Ratios on Cyclic Quadrilaterals**
A,B,C,D is cyclic. P point on its circumcircle. 
$$P(A,B;C,D)=\pm \frac{CA*DB}{CB*DA}$$
(where the sign is positive if AB and CD do not intersect, and negative otherwise.)
