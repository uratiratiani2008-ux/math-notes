$a,b\in\mathbb{Z}$ are coprime, $\forall n\geq1, n\in\mathbb{Z}, \quad \exists p\in\mathbb{P}$ (primitive prime divisor),
for which $p|a^n-b^n$ and $p\nmid a^k-b^k \quad \forall k<n$. 
exceptions:
+ $n=1, a-b=1$
+ $n=2, a+b=2^x$
+ $n=6, a=2, b=1$

Similarly, $a^n+b^n$ has at least one primitive prime divisor.
exceptions:
+ n = 3, a = 2, b = 1
